Pacific West Region: A Big and Small Version of Half Dome



ANALYSIS: The Pacific West Region has a number of national parks that are known internationally. For example, the most visited national park in the country is the Golden Gate National Recreation Area, which includes the iconic view of the Golden Gate Bridge surrounded by the cold Pacific Ocean and lush rolling hills to take on a refreshing hike. I currently live in California and have enjoyed many of the Pacific West Region's national parks. While some may love hiking up Yosemite Park's Half Dome, I am partial to Death Valley for its vast and varying terrains. As we have seen in the other visualizations, the Pacific West Region has also been negatively affected by COVID:19. From the chart, 2019 resembles the mountain Half Dome where there is a increase of visits as the weather starts to warm and then decline in visits after July, when visits are at its highest (almost 8 million July 2019, but drops to a little over 6 million July 2020). With the exception of April 2020 when it looks like the National Parks may have closed its doors to the majority of the public, the 2020 visits dropped but not substantially like the Alaska or National Capital Regions when compared to 2019. This chart looks like to a big and small version of Half Dome.
