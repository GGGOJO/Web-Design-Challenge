Northeast Region: New England Parks Also Hit Hard



ANALYSIS: While I would have guessed that either the Liberty Bell in Independence National Park or the majestic freedom symbol of the Statue of Liberty in New York City would have been the top visited parks in the Northeast Region, but I was pleasantly wrong. Spaning over 27,000 acres from Sandy Hook, NJ to New York City, NY, The Gateway National Recreation Area is "Many Places, One Park" for its beaches and green spaces. While the are popular parks to visits during times with no viral pandemic, the Northeast Region like the other regions analyzed in this project was not immune to the COVID19 restrictions on visiting public places and crowds. The 2019 visitation peak is June (see brown line's peak), with a combined total regional park visit of almost 9 million. Compared to June 2020, that number hit 4 million visits. The gap between the brown and green lines can be quickly measured with the number of grid boxes. Between the months of June and July, we see almost four full grid boxes between the two lines or a drop of approximately 4 million visitors.
