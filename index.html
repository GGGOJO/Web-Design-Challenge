COVID:19 v. US Parks Analysis

PURPOSE: This project began as an exploratory data analysis (EDA) to let the data 'speak to us.' Presented in this website are data analysis on the impact of the novel Coronavirus (COVID:19) on the U.S. National Park Visitations.

BACKGROUND: Data used in this analysis came from the U.S. National Park Service statistics page (NPS Stats), which houses a rich collection of data on visitors to the U.S. National Parks. The data are provided in pre-formatted reports dating back to 1904 and cut by a variety of park charateristics (e.g., park type, park region, state location, acitivities, etc.).

RESEARCH QUESTION: To what extent did COVID:19 affect the park visitation numbers at the regional level before and during COVID:19 lock-down measures and policies took place? For the U.S., many states issued a stay-at-home lock-down policy around March 2020, when COVID:19 began to make its presence on the U.S. population. I present my portion of the research project that answers this research question at a regional level, which examines the 2019 and 2020 data.

TAKEAWAY:

  • The parks did not have similar number of visits in 2020 like 2019, which was expected. Rather, the parks experienced a great decline in visits.
  • Without Googling the answer, apparently the National Park Service closed the parks in the month of April 2020. Depsite closing, there were a still a large number of visits to parks, which I did not expect.
  • These data visualizations help interested stakeholders to understand when guests typically visit a park under non-pandemic conditions. Having these visuals provide a quick glance of when parks are the most busiest. Thus, it tells visitors when to avoid the crowds or the parks when they need to amp up provisions and safety precautions for accommodate large influx of visitors.
